What is an API?

Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (API) is a substance in a finished pharmaceutical product, which has a pharmacological activity or is responsible for generating a direct effect in the diagnosis, treatment or prevention of a disease.

API synthesis is a complex multi-step process that influences the process, cost, and level of impurities in the final API. API research at Emcure Pharmaceuticals focuses on synthesizing molecules that stay true to the commitment of delivering high quality and cost efficient healthcare to the customers. Emcure’s API is focused on complex molecules including those with chiral centers and those requiring development of new science.

Chiral chemistry

Emcure is recognized as the pioneer in chiral chemistry. It has effectively developed and marketed 11 chiral molecules which have demonstrated greater effectiveness, safety and require lesser dosage than their non-chiral counterparts. Examples: S(-) Amlodipine Besilate, S (-)Metoprolol Succinate, Dexrabeprazole Sodium, S(-) Etodolac, Dexketoprofen Trometamol.

What is chirality? In chemistry chirality a molecule will have 2 forms that are mirror images of each other but otherwise identical. These mirror images are non-superimposable on each other. In chemistry terms these are known as enantiomers. This is the simplest form of chirality. The following video is a useful illustration on chirality.
https://youtu.be/JS-iAuCIexk

Pharmacology responds differently to chiral molecules in a lot of cases and thus having a chiral version of a certain biological agent is considered beneficial in most cases.

What are some advantages of a chiral drug?
  • Simplifies basic interpretation of pharmacology
  • Greater selectivity for their biological targets
  • Improved therapeutic index
  • Reduced adverse effects
  • Longer or shorter duration of action – more appropriate dosing frequency
  • Decreased inter individual variability
Why studying stereo-chemical features are important?
  • Biological activity may reside in one of the enantiomers
  • Enantiomers may show same or similar qualitative and quantitative activities
  • Enantiomers may show similar qualitative but different quantitative activities
  • Enantiomers may show altogether different pharmacological activity

What are peptides?

Peptides are intrinsic signaling molecules for various pharmacological processes. Off late they have become a corner stone for therapeutic intervention. Peptide drugs are complex in nature primarily due to challenges in characterizations and synthesis. As the chain length increases these challenges multiply many-fold.

Approved peptides span a wide therapeutic domain. From preterm labor (Autosiban) to erectile dysfunction (aviptadil) to the drug class of “tide” (dulaglutide, exenatide, etc); the therapeutic span is vast. Some of these drugs live aviptadil has been repurposed for covid19 too.

Synthetically peptides drugs can be assembled via a solution-phase strategy or a solid-support strategy.

What are photochemical reactions?

Photochemical reactions are a set of reactions that uses any form of light energy for a transformation. This can be infrared, visible and or ultraviolet light. Nature uses light source for various transformations. Few common examples are photosynthesis in plants, vitamin D synthesis in humans, conversion of oxygen to ozone in the stratosphere, etc.

Photochemistry can be efficiently used to synthesize various chemical matter, especially drug candidates like steroids, vitamin D derivatives, etc.
